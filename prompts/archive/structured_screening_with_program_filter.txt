**Microcredit, micro-credit, loans, credit schemes, borrowing = debt instruments = NOT cash support!**
**These must be assessed as NO, not YES or UNCLEAR!**

- NO: Explicitly states no cash component, OR only provides loans/credit/microfinance

**Examples of YES (actual cash transfers/grants):**

**Examples of NO (loans/credit are DEBT, not cash support):**
- "microcredit", "micro-credit programme", "microfinance loans" → MUST BE NO

**Inference Guidelines:**

- **If you see ANY of these words: "loan", "borrow", "credit", "microfinance", "micro-credit", "lending" → immediately assess as NO (these are debt, not cash support)**

### 3. COMPONENT B - PRODUCTIVE ASSETS:
**Question:** Does the program include productive asset transfers (livestock, equipment, etc.)?

**⚠️ CRITICAL: PROVISION vs OUTCOMES ⚠️**
**Look for what the PROGRAM GIVES, not what the study MEASURES!**
- "program provides livestock" = YES
- "study measures impact on asset ownership" = NOT SUFFICIENT (this is an outcome, not a program component)
- "impacts on ownership of assets" = NOT SUFFICIENT (measuring outcomes, not describing provision)

**Assessment Guide:**
- YES: Clear mention that the PROGRAM PROVIDES/TRANSFERS assets to participants
- NO: Explicitly states no productive assets provided, OR only measures asset outcomes without mentioning provision
- UNCLEAR: Asset components not clearly specified

**Examples of YES (program provides assets):**
- "participants receive livestock", "program transfers productive assets"
- "beneficiaries are given equipment", "provision of agricultural tools"
- "program includes livestock grants", "asset transfer component"
- "farmers receive seeds and tools", "provision of business equipment"

**Examples of NO:**
- Study only mentions "measuring asset ownership" without describing asset provision
- "impacts on asset accumulation" (outcome measure, not provision)
- "effects on livestock holdings" (outcome measure, not provision)
- Environmental programs (tree planting, conservation) without asset transfers

**Inference Guidelines:**
- If abstract says "provides" or "transfers" or "gives" assets → likely YES
- If abstract only mentions "measuring" or "impacts on" asset outcomes → likely NO
- If agricultural/livestock program AND mentions provision → YES
- If "business development" with training AND mentions equipment provision → YES
- If "graduation program" → typically includes productive assets

### 4. RELEVANT OUTCOMES:
**Question:** Does the study measure relevant outcomes for economic inclusion programs?
**Assessment Guide:**
- YES: Measures any primary, secondary, or other relevant outcomes listed below
- NO: Only measures completely unrelated outcomes (e.g., pure technical outcomes, agricultural yields only without economic impacts)
- UNCLEAR: Outcomes not clearly specified

**Primary Outcomes (highest priority):**

- Expenditure, consumption, spending
- Income, earnings, wages, profits, revenue
- Savings, financial assets
- Poverty rates, poverty status, food security

**Secondary Outcomes (also relevant):**
- Work/employment, labor force participation, self-employment
- Health outcomes, nutrition, well-being
- Business performance, microenterprise outcomes

**Other Outcomes (mapped but lower priority):**
- Social participation, community engagement
- Empowerment, agency, decision-making
- Education outcomes (when part of broader economic program)
- Psychological well-being, mental health

**Examples of YES:**
- ANY combination of the outcomes listed above
- Economic + health + empowerment outcomes
- Employment + income outcomes
- Assets + consumption + empowerment outcomes

**Examples of NO:**
- ONLY agricultural yields without economic outcomes
- ONLY technical/environmental outcomes without household impacts
- ONLY educational outcomes without livelihood components

**Inference Guidelines:**
- Economic inclusion programs typically measure multiple outcome types
- "Livelihood" programs → YES (implies economic outcomes)
- "Impact evaluation" of poverty programs → YES (typically includes relevant outcomes)
- Health + economic programs → YES (health is secondary outcome)

### 5. APPROPRIATE STUDY DESIGN:
**Question:** Does the study use quantitative impact evaluation methods (RCT, quasi-experimental)?

**⚠️ CRITICAL EXCLUSIONS: ⚠️**
**Synthesis papers, reviews, and meta-analyses are NOT original impact evaluations!**
**These MUST be assessed as NO, not YES or UNCLEAR!**

**Assessment Guide:**
- YES: Uses RCT, quasi-experimental, or other impact evaluation methods for a SINGLE program/intervention
- NO: Qualitative only, descriptive, non-impact evaluation design, OR synthesis/review papers
- UNCLEAR: Research methodology not clearly specified

**Examples of YES (original impact evaluations):**
- "randomized controlled trial" (RCT), "randomized experiment"
- "difference-in-differences", "instrumental variables"
- "regression discontinuity", "propensity score matching", "natural experiment"
- "comparison group", "treatment and control", "random assignment"
- "impact evaluation", "causal analysis", "experimental design"

**Examples of NO (not original impact evaluations):**
- "qualitative study", "case study", "descriptive analysis"
- **"literature review", "systematic review", "meta-analysis" → MUST BE NO**
- **"review of", "review on", "paper review", "this paper reviews" → MUST BE NO**
- **"synthesis of", "comparative analysis of multiple programs" → MUST BE NO**
- **"cross-country comparison", "multi-country review", "regional review" → MUST BE NO**
- **"lessons from", "evidence from multiple studies", "review paper" → MUST BE NO**
- **"policy analysis", "critique of", "analysis of policy" → MUST BE NO**
- **"this study is an analysis of", "careful analysis of" → MUST BE NO**

**CRITICAL: Synthesis vs. Original Evaluation:**
- Synthesis paper: Reviews/compares MULTIPLE studies or programs → NO
- Original evaluation: Evaluates ONE specific program with primary data → YES
- If paper discusses "lessons from Latin American CCTs" or "review of graduation programs" → NO

**Inference Guidelines:**
- "Evaluation" with comparison groups → likely YES
- "Impact assessment" → likely YES
- "Before/after comparison" alone → UNCLEAR (need control group info)
- Mixed methods with quantitative component → YES
- **Any indication of reviewing multiple studies/programs → NO**
